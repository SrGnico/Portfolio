About Me

Gabriel Nicolas Rodriguez Barrio

As a chef , I understood the importance of languages, not just in terms of spoken communication but also the language of food itself. I was fascinated by how different flavors, textures, and ingredients could come together to create a harmonious dish. This fascination translated into my interest in programming languages, where I could use code to create something meaningful and functional.

Furthermore, I have always believed that food plays a significant role in our lives, not just as sustenance but also as a source of joy and pleasure. I firmly believe that creativity and productivity are closely linked to having a satisfied appetite. This belief is reflected in my motto, "I can't think on an empty stomach," which signifies the importance of nourishment and its positive impact on our ability to think, create, and problem-solve effectively.

My transition from being a chef to pursuing programming was driven by my desire for a change, the need to communicate better, my interest in languages and social skills, and my belief in the importance of satisfying appetites and focusing on the little things that enhance the overall experience.
